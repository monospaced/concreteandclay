"I wouldn't want to be part of any club that would have me as a member" Groucho Marx

I have never really got the Marx brothers, preferring Karl to either Groucho, Zippo or the others whose names I have no interest in recalling. However I thought about the quote above on Monday evening as I was turned away from Trash at The End because my friend and I had not made enough effort on the sartorial front. C&C readers will be appalled to know that I was wearing my Milk and Beef hoodie at the time. It led me to ponder on something that I have thought about for many years. Why do I feel like I want to be part of a movement, be it music/youth culture, political or social?

I remember as a 15 year old being identified as a "typical Smiths fan" and feeling absolutely great about that. I would have liked to be a mod or a punk. I felt a great affinity with the boys (and girls) on the North Bank at Highbury and genuinely felt a moment of intense elation seeing Anelka score at Wembley. I guess it's human nature to want to be part of the crowd, to have those shared experiences and I should be happy with my lot, but there's always going to be a part of me that wants more, to merge the individual into the collective. I think Karl might like that.
